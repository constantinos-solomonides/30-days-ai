I put two AI models in a room to design the same system.

Gemini focused on high-level structural trade-offs. Cursor produced three times the detail but lacked the same analytical weight.

This is part of a 30-day experiment in high-intensity AI use.

With the sandbox finally functional, the infrastructure friction is gone. The flow between design and documentation feels natural now.

Merging their outputs produced a result strictly better than either model alone. But the process revealed exactly where human intervention is non-negotiable. My background in teaching and mentoring has been a quiet advantage here—guiding these agents is much like managing an enthusiastic junior colleague who needs clear, direct feedback and ego-free adjustment.

In a review loop, I caught a formatting bug that would have broken the documentation rendering. The AI understood the semantic intent but ignored the presentation behavior. Semantic correctness is not enough for a professional deliverable.

We made 17 technology decisions by applying a simple heuristic: pick the most lightweight option with an official Docker image.

The result is a full implementation guide and a test plan for 48 specific scenarios. No application code has been written yet.

The design exists before the implementation. Having structure before starting ensures the moped MVP actually leads toward the bus we intend to build.
